Business Decisions

We will have five factors. The five factors explain the majority, 55%, of the total variance. Each additional factor after five does not add significant amount of variance explanation. Furthermore, the company will not be able to customize its boats according to more than a few factor characteristics of its customer base. Our choice of factors and analysis are written in the Conclusion section at the end of this report.

Our Approach

Factor Analysis in 6 steps

  1. Confirm the data in metric

  2. Decide whether to scale or standardize the data

  3. Check the correlation matrix to see if Factor Analysis makes sense

  4. Develop a scree plot and decide on the number of factors to be derived

  5. Interpret the factors (consider factor rotations - technical but useful)

  6. Save factor scores for subsequent analyses

Intepretation, Decisions and Conclusion

We named the five factors as follows: 1. status 2. outdoor experience 3. brand, features 4. expertise 5. price conscious

Some issues to consider are that the design of the survey questions may have bias. There may also be variation in the answers, i.e., different survey-takers may have different interpretations for the survey scale numbers. We should run further iterations to strengthen our analysis and refine our choice and description of factors.
